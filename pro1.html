Kerala Cultures and Traditions


Kerala's culture is a blend of traditional arts, vibrant festivals, and unique architecture, deeply connected to its natural environment. Key aspects include its rich performing arts like Kathakali and Mohiniyattam, major festivals such as Onam, and a cuisine focused on rice, coconut, and spices. Traditional architecture incorporates natural materials like wood and often features open courtyards.

Waterfalls in Kerala


Kerala is known for its numerous waterfalls, with notable examples being Athirappilly Falls, the largest in the state, and Palaruvi Falls, meaning "stream of milk". Other popular destinations include Vazhachal Falls, near Athirappilly, and Thusharagiri Waterfalls, which is popular for trekking. The region's many waterfalls contribute to its natural beauty, making it a significant tourist destination.
